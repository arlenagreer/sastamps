About SAPA

Celebrating over 125 years of philatelic tradition in South Texas

Our Mission

The San Antonio Philatelic Association is dedicated to promoting the enjoyment of stamp collecting through education, fellowship, and service to the philatelic community.

Education

We promote philatelic knowledge through presentations, discussions, and our quarterly newsletter.

Fellowship

We foster camaraderie among collectors of all levels of experience and collecting interests.

Service

We serve the broader community through educational outreach and promotion of philately as a hobby.

Our Rich History

1896

Foundation

The San Antonio Philatelic Association was founded by a group of 12 dedicated stamp collectors who began meeting regularly to trade stamps and share knowledge.

1920s

Growth & Expansion

Membership grew to over 50 collectors from throughout South Texas. The club began publishing its first newsletter, originally called "The Collector's Monthly."

1946

Golden Anniversary

SAPA celebrated its 50th anniversary with a special exhibition and banquet. The club received recognition from the American Philatelic Society for its longevity and contributions to the hobby.

1962

First Annual Exhibition

The club organized its first formal annual exhibition, which later became known as SAPA EXPO, showcasing members' collections and attracting visitors from across the state.

1975

PHILATEX Launch

The club's newsletter was revamped and renamed "PHILATEX," which continues to be published quarterly and has won several awards for philatelic journalism.

1996

Centennial Celebration

SAPA celebrated 100 years with a special exhibition featuring rare stamps from members' collections and a commemorative postal cancellation issued by the USPS.

2010s

Digital Transformation

The club embraced technology with the launch of its website, digital newsletter options, and virtual meeting capabilities to complement in-person gatherings.

2021

125th Anniversary

Despite pandemic challenges, SAPA marked its 125th year with virtual events and a special edition of PHILATEX chronicling the club's remarkable journey through American history.

Today

Continuing the Legacy

Today, SAPA continues to thrive with a diverse membership of collectors from all backgrounds, maintaining the same passion for philately that inspired its founding in 1896.
